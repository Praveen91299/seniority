1. Let R denote a random symmetric matrix, where each R_ij are independent normally distributed random variables with mean \mu_ij and standard deviation \sigma_ij, up to the symmetry condition. Let X denote the matrix of means: X_ij = \mu_ij, and let E denote the ground state energy of X. The goal is to obtain an estimate of E, but we only have access to a random matrix R obtained from sampling. Therefore, letting \lambda denote the ground state energy of R (which is a random variable), we need to ask: (1) what is the standard deviation of \lambda, and (2) what is the bias of \lambda as an estimator for E. The notebook `eigenvalue_error_and_bias.ipynb` suggests that there is bias.
